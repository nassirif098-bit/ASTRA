About Earth

What is Earth?

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known astronomical object to harbor life. It's a dynamic world with vast oceans, protective atmosphere, and diverse ecosystems that support millions of species, including humans.

Composition

Earth is composed of an iron-nickel core, rocky mantle and crust, and abundant liquid water. Its atmosphere is 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, with trace amounts of other gases that support life.

Did You Know?

Earth is the only planet not named after a Roman god or goddess. It has a powerful magnetic field that protects life from solar radiation, and 71% of its surface is covered by water.

Structure of Earth

Earth has a complex layered structure:

  • Inner Core : Solid iron-nickel alloy, about 1,220 km radius
  • Outer Core : Liquid iron-nickel, generates magnetic field
  • Mantle : Semi-solid rock, about 2,900 km thick
  • Crust : Solid outer layer, 5-70 km thick
  • Atmosphere : Protective gas layer supporting life
  • Hydrosphere : All of Earth's water systems

The Goldilocks Planet

Earth orbits in the "Goldilocks Zone" - just the right distance from the Sun to maintain liquid water. Its atmosphere regulates temperature, blocks harmful radiation, and provides the perfect conditions for life to flourish.

The planet's tectonic activity recycles minerals and regulates climate, while the Moon stabilizes Earth's axial tilt, creating stable seasons.

Unique Features

  • Oceans : Vast bodies of liquid water covering most of the surface
  • Continents : Seven major land masses with diverse ecosystems
  • Atmosphere : Perfect blend of gases for breathing and protection
  • Moon : Large natural satellite stabilizing climate
  • Magnetic Field : Protective shield against solar radiation
  • Plate Tectonics : Constantly reshaping the surface

Earth Timeline

4.5 Billion Years Ago

Earth forms from accretion of cosmic dust and debris in the early Solar System.

4.4 Billion Years Ago

First oceans form as water vapor condenses, creating the primordial seas.

3.8 Billion Years Ago

First life appears in the form of single-celled organisms in the oceans.

540 Million Years Ago

Cambrian Explosion brings rapid diversification of complex life forms.

66 Million Years Ago

Mass extinction event wipes out dinosaurs, allowing mammals to dominate.

200,000 Years Ago

Modern humans (Homo sapiens) first appear in Africa.
