Galaxy Vibes

Milky Way

Our home galaxy, a majestic barred spiral that harbors our solar system among hundreds of billions of stars, planets, and cosmic wonders scattered across the vast expanse of space.

Galactic Rotation

The Milky Way spins like a giant cosmic vinyl, taking about 230 million years for one full turn around its center.

Age of the Milky Way

This galaxy is around 13.6 billion years old, basically ancient by universe standards and almost as old as the cosmos itself.

Types of Stars

It's packed with everything from tiny red dwarfs to massive supergiants lighting up the spiral arms with their brilliant glow.

Fun Facts

Our solar system is drifting through space at roughly 220 km/s (828,000 km/h), and we don't even feel it!

Andromeda Galaxy

Our closest major cosmic neighbor, Andromeda (M31) is a majestic spiral galaxy visible to the naked eye from Earth. It's heading our way for a spectacular galactic merger in a few billion years.

Colossal Size

Andromeda stretches approximately 220,000 light-years in diameter, making it more than twice the size of the Milky Way.

Stellar Richness

It contains about 1 trillion stars, roughly 2 to 3 times more than our own galaxy's stellar population.

Distance from Earth

Located 2.5 million light-years away from us, it's the most distant object visible to the naked eye from Earth.

Future Collision

Andromeda is approaching us at 110 km/s! In 4.5 billion years, it will merge with the Milky Way to form a giant elliptical galaxy.
