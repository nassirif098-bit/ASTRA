Mercury

The Swift Messenger of the Gods

About Mercury

What is Mercury?

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System, orbiting closest to the Sun. It's a rocky terrestrial planet with extreme temperature variations and no substantial atmosphere.

Composition

Mercury is composed primarily of iron (about 70%) and silicate materials (about 30%), making it one of the most iron-rich planets in the Solar System.

Did You Know?

A day on Mercury lasts 59 Earth days while its year is only 88 Earth days. This means Mercury has the most eccentric orbit of all the planets!

Structure of Mercury

Mercury has a unique internal structure:

  • Core : Massive iron core occupying about 85% of the planet's radius
  • Mantle : Thin silicate mantle surrounding the core
  • Crust : Rocky surface covered with craters and cliffs
  • Exosphere : Extremely thin atmosphere of oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen

Extreme Environment

Mercury experiences the most extreme temperature variations in the Solar System, ranging from 430°C (800°F) during the day to -180°C (-290°F) at night.

Its proximity to the Sun and lack of atmosphere create these harsh conditions, making it one of the most challenging planets to study.

Surface Features

  • Impact Craters : Heavily cratered surface from asteroid impacts
  • Scarps : Massive cliffs formed as the planet cooled and contracted
  • Smooth Plains : Volcanic plains filling large impact basins
  • Polar Ice : Water ice in permanently shadowed craters at the poles

Mercury Timeline

4.5 Billion Years Ago

Mercury forms from the solar nebula, developing its massive iron core.

3.8 Billion Years Ago

Late Heavy Bombardment period creates Mercury's heavily cratered surface.

1974-1975

Mariner 10 becomes the first spacecraft to visit Mercury, mapping 45% of its surface.

2011-2015

MESSENGER orbiter completes the first detailed map of Mercury's entire surface.
