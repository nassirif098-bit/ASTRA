BLACK HOLES

The Universe's Most Mysterious Phenomena

What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing—no particles or even electromagnetic radiation such as light—can escape from it. The theory of general relativity predicts that a sufficiently compact mass can deform spacetime to form a black hole.

Types of Black Holes

Stellar Black Holes

3-100 Solar Masses

Formed when massive stars collapse at the end of their life cycle. These are the most common type of black holes.

Supermassive Black Holes

Millions to Billions Solar Masses

Found at the centers of galaxies, including our own Milky Way. Sagittarius A* has 4 million solar masses.

Intermediate Black Holes

100-100,000 Solar Masses

Rare and mysterious, these mid-sized black holes bridge the gap between stellar and supermassive types.

Primordial Black Holes

Variable Sizes

Hypothetical black holes formed shortly after the Big Bang due to density fluctuations in the early universe.

Anatomy of a Black Hole

Event Horizon

The boundary beyond which nothing can escape. Also called the "point of no return."

Singularity

The core of the black hole where matter is crushed to infinite density and gravity becomes infinite.

Accretion Disk

A disk of gas and matter spiraling into the black hole, heated to millions of degrees.

Jets

Powerful streams of particles ejected at near light-speed perpendicular to the accretion disk.

Mind-Bending Facts

01

Time slows down near a black hole due to extreme gravity—a phenomenon called gravitational time dilation.

02

If you fell into a black hole, you'd experience "spaghettification"—being stretched like a noodle.

03

The first image of a black hole was captured in 2019, showing the supermassive black hole in M87 galaxy.

04

Black holes don't actually "suck"—they attract matter just like any massive object, but their gravity is extreme.

05

Stephen Hawking predicted that black holes emit radiation and can eventually evaporate over trillions of years.

06

The closest known black hole to Earth is about 1,000 light-years away in the constellation Telescopium.
